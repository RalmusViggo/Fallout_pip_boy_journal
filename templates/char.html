Recurring Characters

  • The Vault Dweller

  • Whooee this is probably one of the most substantial one, i have personally met him, but this was before i found this pip-boy, here's about him, oh and all this i have gotten from him:

    • The Vault Dweller was one of hundreds of inhabitants of Vault 13. He was raised by the community and robotic caretakers, leading an unremarkable life in the Vault. His journey began when the water-purification chip the Vault relied on to create fresh water broke down. Without spare parts or the possibility of a workaround system, the overseer had to send an individual outside the Vault to seek a new one out. With no alternative, the Vault 13 overseer gathered everyone between certain ages, healthy enough to venture out, and made them draw straws. The Vault Dweller pulled out the short one and was sent out to find a replacement chip. He left the Vault the next day, on December 5, 2161.

    The Vault Dweller struggled for the first few days, fighting off rats and the elements. With only the location of Vault 15 to guide him, he started out eastwards. On his way, he happened upon the small settlement of Shady Sands. The Vault Dweller aided them and earned their trust. Tandi and Aradesh in turned helped him, sharing their knowledge. After hiring Ian, the Vault Dweller continued on to the buried Vault. Exploring the ruins, he realized that it would not help as the control room and the water chip were buried under tons of rock, so the duo moved on.

    A clue he acquired in the city led them to Necropolis, the city of ghouls. There, he fought strange hulking creatures known as super mutants, guarding the watershed. The Vault Dweller was devastated to lose Ian, killed by a mutant's flamer, burning him to death. The memory of his burning flesh would forever be etched into the Dweller's mind. However, Ian's sacrifice was not in vain, as the Vault Dweller found the water chip and returned with it to Vault 13.

    The happiness of his return was marred by the report he logged with the archives. The overseer ran calculations and determined that the kind of mutants witnessed by the Vault Dweller at Necropolis were too much of a threat to ignore. With his knowledge of the mutants, the overseer sent the Vault Dweller out again

    To cut a VERY long story short, he took down the unity, the master, and the master's secret F.E.V lab.

  • The Chosen One

  • A member of the tribe of Arroyo, as a child of the tribe's elder, they are also the grandchild of the Vault Dweller. They gain their epithet from being the one chosen to rescue their village from a historical drought by seeking the long-lost Vault 13 and recovering a G.E.C.K.

    • The bloodline of the wasteland's second-most influential hero extends all the way back to the first, the Vault Dweller. As a child of the elder of Arroyo, the daughter of the Vault Dweller and his wife Pat, along with at least one unnamed sibling (who sired their nephew Feargus), the Chosen One is a member of the third generation of descendants from those who once lived in Vault 13, similar to how their grandparent was the third generation to live inside the Vault.

    The Chosen One was raised in the tribal village of Arroyo and trained from childhood to become its champion and future elder, undergoing spiritual trials guided by the shaman Hakunin before completing the Temple of Trials. When a devastating drought threatened the village, their mother sent them into the wasteland to find a Garden of Eden Creation Kit (G.E.C.K.), a device believed to restore the land. During their journey, the Chosen One influenced many communities—helping towns like Klamath and Modoc, forming alliances with figures such as John Cassidy and the mutant sheriff Marcus, exposing conspiracies in Broken Hills, and affecting the politics of places like New Reno and the New California Republic by defeating the New Khans and supporting peaceful expansion. Their quest eventually revealed a far greater threat: the Enclave had kidnapped the people of Arroyo to develop a biological weapon meant to wipe out most of humanity. The Chosen One infiltrated their forces, fought through their installations, and ultimately destroyed the Enclave Oil Rig, killing President Dick Richardson and the powerful agent Frank Horrigan while rescuing the captives. With the G.E.C.K., the survivors of Arroyo and Vault 13 founded a new settlement together, where the Chosen One became leader and guided the community into a thriving civilization, leaving behind a legacy remembered across the wasteland.

  • The Lone Wanderer

  • The Lone Wanderer was born in the Jefferson Memorial to scientists James and Catherine, who were working on Project Purity, a plan to cleanse the region’s water of radiation; however, Catherine died shortly after childbirth, prompting James to abandon the project and flee with his child to Vault 101. Raised in the safety of the vault, the Lone Wanderer grew up alongside friends like Amata and lived an ordinary life until James suddenly disappeared, forcing them to leave the vault and search the ruins of Washington, D.C. Following his trail across the wasteland, the Lone Wanderer aided settlements such as Megaton and eventually found James, only for their reunion to end when the Enclave seized Project Purity, leading James to sacrifice himself to prevent it from falling into their hands. Determined to finish their father’s work, the Lone Wanderer allied with the Brotherhood of Steel, retrieved a G.E.C.K. from Vault 87, escaped Enclave captivity at Raven Rock, and helped lead an assault on the Jefferson Memorial with the giant robot Liberty Prime to reclaim the purifier. After defeating Colonel Autumn, the purifier was finally activated despite deadly radiation, completing the dream of providing clean water to the wasteland. Surviving the ordeal, the Lone Wanderer later helped the Brotherhood distribute the purified water and destroyed the Enclave’s remaining forces by attacking their stronghold at Adams Air Force Base, while their travels also carried them through many other strange and dangerous adventures across the Capital Wasteland and beyond.

  • The Courier/Courier Six

  • * Courier Six and i were actually quite good friends back in the day, but i could never figure out their gender... they were quite ambiguous and never took of their helmet. *

    • The Courier was a well-traveled messenger who spent years crossing New California, working caravan routes, running deliveries, and occasionally taking on other jobs, gaining experience with machinery and the harsh realities of the wasteland. During one earlier delivery, they unknowingly transported a device that later triggered the detonation of buried nuclear missiles beneath a settlement near Death Valley, destroying the community and turning the region into the devastated wasteland known as the Divide—an event the Courier left unaware of at the time. Later, they were hired by the Mojave Express to carry a package to New Vegas, a delivery that secretly involved the highly valuable platinum chip. Near Goodsprings, the Courier was ambushed by Benny and a group of Great Khans, who stole the chip and shot the Courier in the head before burying them in a shallow grave. Miraculously surviving, the Courier was dug up by a Securitron named Victor and treated by the local doctor in Goodsprings, allowing them to recover and begin their journey across the Mojave to track down Benny, recover the platinum chip, and shape the fate of New Vegas. Along the way, fragments of the Courier’s past suggest a life of wandering and varied experience—possibly including bounty hunting, con artistry, mechanical work, and deep knowledge of wasteland factions and technology—while later events even left them partially augmented with advanced cybernetics after surviving extraordinary dangers.

  • The Master

  • "The Unity will bring about the master race. Master! Master! One able to survive, or even thrive, in the wasteland. As long as there are differences, we will tear ourselves apart fighting each other. We need one race. Race! Race! One goal. Goal! Goal! One people . . . to move forward to our destiny. Destiny." - The Master

  • "So, what shall it be? Do you join the Unity or do you die here? Join! Die! Join! Die!" - The Master

    • The Master of the Super Mutants or simply the Master, referred to by the Children of the Cathedral as the Holy Flame, Dark God, and Father Hope, was the founder and leader of the Unity, a mutant-led organization dedicated to the post-humanist transformation of mankind in the post-nuclear world. By the time he was encountered by the Vault Dweller, the Master was a hybridized amalgamation of several dozens of humans and other mutants, as well as the Cathedral's computer network, speaking in multiple voices and switching between them on the fly.

    The Master's end goal was to create a new race from humanity using FEV, one that could thrive in the post-apocalyptic conditions of the world and to rid humanity of its differences and reasons for waging war. The Master's race of mutants would be dubbed super mutants colloquially by most and would become a permanent presence throughout California and neighboring areas and a long-lasting reminder of the Master's attempt to reform humanity.

    Born Richard Moreau, he would later change his name to Richard Grey while still human. A variety of names used to refer to him by his followers, including Holy Flame, Dark God, Father Hope and others. The Master is the main antagonist of Fallout, encountered at the Cathedral. Destroying him is required to eliminate the mutant threat, although meeting the Master in person is entirely optional.
