Yao Guai

So imagine the deathclaw, but in mamal form, and that's the yao guai! these big bears deal more damage than a normal deathclaw, but aren't as resiliant.

Yao guai are the mutated descendants of the American black bear. These vicious creatures are found in Appalachia, the Capital Wasteland, the Commonwealth, the Island, and Zion Canyon.

Background

Named by descendants of those held in Chinese internment camps before the Great War, yao guai are mutated American black bears. They are one of the more vicious creatures of the wasteland, having razor sharp claws and teeth, and a nasty temperament.

Characteristics

Yao guai are large, mutated ursines that are descended from the bears that lived across America before the Great War. They are able to reproduce, as evidenced by the presence of cubs. Wild yao guai will attack nearly any other animals or people they encounter, including deathclaws. Despite this, yao guai exhibit a gentle, familial bond toward one another when found together and can even be domesticated.

Yao guai may be hunted for their meat and hides. Yao guai may be prepared into ribs and roasts. Yao guai remains may be kept as trophies, trinkets or used as materials to make exotic weapons, such as Bear Arm or She's Embrace.

Biology

East Coast variant

The physical build of the East Coast variant does not seem to drastically deviate from pre-War bears as much as the yao guai in the Capital Wasteland and Zion Canyon. Comparatively, they are overall less dog-like in their posture and movement and sport thicker patches of fur and more body fat. Since their upper body, chest and arms are not as massive, the Appalachian yao guai have a more traditional bear-like posture and gait. These yao guai are generally larger than their unmutated predecessors and are more aggressive.

Zion Canyon variant

The physical build of the Zion variant does not drastically deviate from pre-War bears as much as other cases of yao guai found in the Capital Wasteland and the Commonwealth. Comparatively, they are overall less dog-like in their posture and movement and sport thicker patches of fur and more body fat. Additionally, since their upper body, chest and arms are not as massive, the Appalachian yao guai have a comparatively more traditional bear-like posture and gait. Yao guai are generally larger than their unmutated predecessors and more aggressive.

Attributes

Yao guai are extremely fast and powerful creatures, second only to deathclaws in terms of raw strength and speed. Unlike deathclaws, they are less alert, as you may come in contact with one when it is asleep or lying down. Strangely enough, Yao guai do not attack bighorners in Zion Canyon. They will become hostile towards you upon detection.

Yao Guai variants

East Coast Variants

Stunted yao guai

Though not as physically imposing as normal yao guai due to minor malformations, past injuries, or arrested development. As a result, they are less of a threat compared to other variants, but these bears are still formidable opponents. Wherever they are found with another yao guai it is usually one with a similar affliction.

Yao guai

The typical form of a yao guai, they are stronger than their stunted counterparts and can prove a difficult fight for low level player characters due to their considerable strength and aggressive nature.

Yao guai ghoul

A ghoulified version of the yao guai. I have only encountered these in Far Harbor. Its sensory organs seem to be heavily damaged due to radiation poisoning, age and past injuries, as they hare less perceptive than their non-ghoulified variant.

Though they are less perceptive and look more... dead, these ghoulsih bears are more dangerous, they deal more damage and take more hits to go down.
